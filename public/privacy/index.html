Privacy Policy

Effective 23 July 2026

The short version: No accounts. No ads. No tracking cookies. We keep an anonymous count of how often each game is played and finished — no names, no profiles, nothing tied to you. Almost everything else (your scores, streaks, and settings) stays in your own browser and is never sent anywhere.

Wordems is a small collection of daily browser puzzle games at wordems.com. This page explains what information the site does and doesn't handle, in plain language.

What stays on your device

Your play data lives in your browser's local storage, on your device. We don't receive it (unless you turn on supporter sync — see below). This includes:

  • Which puzzles you've completed, your scores, and your streaks;
  • Your chosen colour theme and other preferences;
  • Supporter status and, if you have one, your license key;
  • A small flag used to avoid double-counting you in the anonymous stats below.

Clearing your browser's site data for wordems.com removes all of it.

Anonymous usage stats

To see which games people enjoy and which need work, the site keeps a simple daily tally per game: how many devices opened it, finished a round, and finished all difficulties. When one of those happens, your browser sends a single message containing only the date, the game, and the event — for example { "day": "2026-07-23", "game": "rootword", "event": "finish" }.

  • No identifier of any kind is sent — no name, no account, no cookie, and we don't store your IP address.
  • It's de-duplicated on your device, so each game/event is counted at most once per day. That means we're counting devices, never people, and we can't link one day to another.
  • It covers today's live puzzles only — replaying an old puzzle from the archive doesn't count.
  • It's off automatically if your browser sends a "Do Not Track" or Global Privacy Control signal, and you can turn it off yourself with the Anonymous play stats switch at the bottom of the home screen.

If you become a supporter

Supporting Wordems is optional and unlocks a few extras (colour themes, the puzzle archive, and cross-device sync). Payment is handled entirely by our reseller, Lemon Squeezy, who act as the merchant of record. They collect your payment details and email to process the purchase — we never see your card details. After a purchase we store your license key and the email Lemon Squeezy passes to us, solely to unlock your perks and, if you enable it, to sync your progress.

Cross-device sync (supporters only)

If you turn on sync, your puzzle history — dates, scores, and which difficulty, but none of your personal information — is stored on our server (Cloudflare D1), keyed by your license key. Its only purpose is to restore your progress when you play on another device. Turn off sync or contact us to have it deleted.

Cookies

We use no advertising or tracking cookies. The site uses your browser's local storage for the things described above, which is not shared across sites. Lemon Squeezy's checkout, on their own domain, may use their own cookies as described in their policy.

Who else is involved

  • Cloudflare — hosts the site and its small backend.
  • Lemon Squeezy — processes supporter payments.

There are no advertising networks, no Google Analytics, and no social media trackers.

Your choices

  • Turn the anonymous stats off with the switch on the home screen, or via your browser's Do Not Track / Global Privacy Control setting.
  • Clear all locally stored data any time through your browser settings.
  • Ask us to delete supporter data tied to your license key — see Contact.

Children

Wordems is a general-audience site and isn't directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect information from them.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update this page and the effective date above.
